How It All Began

A Community Saw a Need

Every great mission begins with a moment of recognition. For Meals on Wheels Pasco, that moment came when a group of compassionate local residents recognized a quiet, growing challenge: our elderly and homebound neighbors were struggling to maintain their independence simply because they lacked regular, nutritious meals.

On February 21, 1974, East Pasco Meals on Wheels, Inc. was officially founded to answer that call. What began as a modest, grassroots movement has grown into a lifeline for the eastern portion of the county.

Our Journey
  • 1974 — Founded
    On February 21, 1974, East Pasco Meals on Wheels, Inc. was officially founded to answer that call. What began as a modest, grassroots movement has grown into a lifeline for the eastern portion of the county.
  • 1978 — Growing Strong
    Fifth Annual Meeting held at the Zephyrhills Community Building. Marking a pivotal transition from a startup initiative to a permanent community fixture, this year solidified our role as a primary lifeline for homebound residents throughout Pasco County.
  • 1983 — Foundation for the Future
    East Pasco Meals on Wheels, Inc. incorporated as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Community-funded from day one — no government funding accepted.
  • 2021 — A New Chapter
    On March 20, we celebrated the grand opening of the Genesis Community Center. More than just a kitchen, this expansion allowed us to offer local residents a place for social activities, wellness programs, and community connection.
  • 2025 — Feeding the Heart of Pasco
    The Feeding You With Love Food Pantry is created — serving any Pasco County resident in need, not just those on delivery routes.
  • NOW
    Today — A Legacy of Service
    Currently serving 1,000+ neighbors every week, we deliver tens of thousands of meals annually across Pasco County. Our mission continues to evolve, ensuring that no resident is left behind and that every meal comes with a smile and a wellness check.

Our Unwavering Commitment

From our inception, we established a bold and defining philosophy: we are a non-profit, community-funded organization. To this day, we maintain our independence by never accepting government funding.

Our success is built entirely on the bedrock of local generosity — donations from small businesses, dedicated churches, community grants, and the tireless spirit of local volunteers.
